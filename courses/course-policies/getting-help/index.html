Getting Help

You can ask questions about anything at any time without judgment.

Community Discussion

All asynchronous course discussions will occur in the Community discussion forum on Blackboard. This is a private discussion forum and only students in our class can participate in it. However, this forum is not a place to discuss personal information such as grades or private concerns - that would be best to email me about. I will be available to assist you, but your peers will also be a great resource. I encourage you to seek help in ways that are visible to others so that all can benefit.

One of our first tasks will be to create a post to help you become comfortable with this process.

Class Sessions

You may prefer to get your questions answered live. Each section of STA 216 will meet synchronously in live sessions through Blackboard Collaborate this semester. These sessions will be recorded and available to all students in that section afterwards. Leading up to these sessions, I will solicit additional items beyond what I have planned to cover through the Community discussion forum on Blackboard. If you post items to this forum at least two hours prior to the session, I will do my best to plan a brief demonstration, lesson, etc. covering them. Live sessions are an excellent opportunity to ask questions about course material, assignments, or other course details.

Attendance

Again, each class session will be live-streamed so that you can participate remotely. It is critical that you actually attend these sessions. Note that GVSU has strengthened its attendance policy for Winter 2021 that states that you are expected to be in attendance at synchronous online meetings.

Individual Sessions

You are also welcome to meet with me individually (or bring along your team members). Individual sessions are an excellent opportunity for you to ask questions about graduate schools, research opportunities, or careers in statistics or data science. Additionally, during individual sessions you can ask me about your grades or other personal items.

Due to public health concerns, all student meetings this semester will be conducted remotely unless you have a specific need that requires a physical meeting outside of class. Email me to let me know that you would like to meet in-person and we will arrange a socially distanced-meeting.

I typically check email between 7 am and 5 pm on weekdays and occasionally during the weekend. I do my best to respond promptly to any email, but please do not expect an immediate response. I may need up to 6 hours to respond depending on my schedule and the length of my response.

Student Drop-In Hours

Monday, Tuesday, and Thursday from 1:00 – 2:00 pm I will be available online for drop-in visitors. A Google Meet video conference link has been shared with you on Blackboard where you can access these sessions. You do not need an appointment to attend. If you cannot attend these Student Drop-In Hours, you can contact me by email or by scheduling an appointment in the Google Calendar that is shared with you on Blackboard.
